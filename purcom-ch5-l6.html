Writing a Political Analysis Paper

Communication for Academic Purposes

Academic writing involves structured, evidence-based communication. From avoiding plagiarism to writing proposals. Today, we focus on Lesson 6.

What is a Political Analysis Paper?

It goes beyond summarizing events. It evaluates policies, political behaviors, power dynamics, and institutional systems to understand why and how decisions are made.

Why Political Analysis in Nursing?

Healthcare is inherently political. Funding, hospital regulations, Medicare, scope of practice, and public health responses are all dictated by political decisions and policies.

Nurses as Advocates

To advocate effectively for patients, nurses must understand the political landscape. A political analysis helps nurses dissect laws like the Nurse Staffing Standards for Hospital Patient Safety and Quality Care Act.

Requirements for a Decent Paper

1. Clear Thesis

A strong, arguable position on a political issue.

2. Empirical Evidence

Data, statistics, and historical context to back claims.

3. Logical Structure

A cohesive flow from introduction to conclusion.

Requirement 1: The Thesis Statement

Your paper needs a central argument. It shouldn't just be "Nursing shortages are bad." It must be an analytical claim about the politics behind the issue.

Example of a Nursing-Focused Thesis

"This paper argues that the continued failure to pass federal mandated nurse-to-patient ratios is driven by the lobbying power of corporate hospital associations, resulting in systemic risks to public health outcomes."

Requirement 2: Empirical Evidence

Political analysis relies on facts, not just opinions. You must use verifiable data to prove your thesis.

Finding Evidence in Nursing Policy

  • Peer-reviewed journals (e.g., Journal of Nursing Regulation)
  • Government health statistics (CDC, WHO, DOH)
  • Legislative tracking (Bills, voting records)
  • Reports from Nursing Unions (ANA, NNU)

Requirement 3: Objectivity & Counter-arguments

A decent political analysis acknowledges opposing views. What does the other side say? Analyzing counter-arguments strengthens your own position.

Addressing the Counter-Argument

Example: While nurses argue for mandated ratios for safety, hospital administrators argue that rigid ratios cause financial strain and force unit closures.

Your paper must analyze both political pressures.

Requirement 4: Structure

Introduction Body Conclusion

The Introduction

1. The Hook

Engage the reader with a stark reality.

2. The Context

Briefly explain the policy or political situation.

3. The Thesis

State your core argument clearly.

The Body Paragraphs

Each paragraph should tackle one specific point that supports your thesis.

Point 1: Lobbying impacts
Point 2: Patient mortality data
Point 3: Economic cost vs benefit

The Conclusion

Do not just summarize. Synthesize.

Restate your thesis in a new way, summarize your findings, and end with a "So What?" (Why does this matter for the future?)

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Writing a history report instead of an analysis.
  • Letting personal bias override objective evidence.
  • Failing to format citations properly.

Empowering the Profession

Writing a political analysis paper isn't just an academic exercise. It is training for real-world nursing advocacy.

Writing well means advocating well.

Lesson 6 Summary

Political Analysis evaluates policy, power, and outcomes.

Requires a strong thesis, empirical evidence, and clear structure.

Highly relevant to Nursing for advocating health policy reforms.
